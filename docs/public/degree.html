Computer Engineering

This degree program is designed to provide students with a strong foundation in the field of computer engineering. It includes a well-rounded curriculum covering both theoretical and practical aspects of the discipline.

Purpose

The purpose of this degree is to equip students with the skills and knowledge required to design, develop, and maintain computer systems, applications, and networks.

Structure

The program is structured into multiple modules, each focusing on key areas of computer engineering. Students will gain hands-on experience and theoretical insights to prepare them for professional careers or further academic pursuits.

Study Plan for Computer Engineering.

First Cuatrimester

  • Fundamentals of Computer Engineering

    Click on here if you want an in-depth exploration of its content, objectives, and learning outcomes.

  • Administration of Operating Systems I

    The course Administration of Operating Systems I introduces students to the role of the operating system within a computer through the study of its main functions. It presents basic file management techniques and trains students in the installation, basic configuration, and operation of operating systems.

    The course covers an introduction to operating systems, focusing on fundamental elements such as what an operating system is, its functions and objectives, as well as the installation, use, and basic configuration of the most widely used operating systems.

  • Knowledge Management and Person-Centered Soft Skills

    This course on Knowledge Management and Person-Centered Soft Skills is an opportunity for freshmen students to set up the foundations needed to make their university journey something extraordinary.

    The purpose of the course is to deepen the student's knowledge of themselves, recognizing the habits that must be trained in the different dimensions of the person, in order to promote personal maturity. Our aim is for students to discover and personally confront the meaning of their university experience and it's impact in their life, as well as the value of university culture: community search for knowledge, sensitivity towards theory and the deployment of creative actions, comprehensive training and personal and social leadership

  • Introduction to Cybersecurity

    The ultimate goal is for each student to have a basic understanding of the main trends and elements that shape the world of Cybersecurity, both for businesses and end consumers. This will enable them to delve deeper into each of the aspects that make up cybersecurity.

  • Logic and Critical Thinking

    The objective is to develop the ability to reason and think rigorously, helping students maximize their learning, identify as seekers of truth, and prepare to face personal and professional challenges. Additionally, it focuses on shaping leaders capable of bridging data analysis with strategic decision-making, addressing the growing demand for talent in this field.

  • Discrete Mathematics

    The course Discrete Mathematics has as its main objectives the application of set theory and graph theory, the management of finite and modular arithmetic, the use of logical and recursive methods to formalize reasoning systematically, the demonstration of the existence of properties of infinite sets through mathematical induction, and finally, the synthesis of the generation of an infinite number of elements into a finite set of rules in a recursive manner.

  • Mathematics for Engineering I

    This course promotes the practice of analytical, synthetic, reflective, critical, theoretical, and practical reasoning, with its ultimate goal being to discover and model reality. Through serious, rigorous, and passionate work, it leads students toward scientific research.

    The objective of the course is to equip the student with sufficient mathematical skills to tackle common problems in computer engineering, understanding their cross-disciplinary nature and following a process of orientation, representation, decision, and action that leads to finding the solution.

Second Cuatrimester

  • Object-Oriented Programming

    The primary goal of this course is to enable students to master the analysis, design, implementation, and testing of software using the Object-Oriented Programming (OOP) paradigm. Through this comprehensive approach, students will become proficient in utilizing methods, techniques, and tools that are integral to engineering robust and maintainable applications. A special emphasis will be placed on the implementation phase, with a focus on the practical application of concepts through the C# programming as the primary language ensurings that students not only understand OOP principles but also how to effectively apply them in real-world scenarios.

  • Administration of Operating Systems II

    The course covers the introduction to Unix-based operating systems, including their installation and partitioning, shell management, basic/advanced commands, and their objectives.

    The student will learn the basic functioning of a Unix-based operating system, starting with its installation and configuration, and continuing with system administration through shell commands.

  • Electronics and Computer Technology

    The course aims to generate in the student an attitude of amazement and curiosity for the physical reality that underlies the computer systems, promoting an intellectual desire for the seek of knowledge and the habit of wondering about the truth of the things we all relate to. It also seeks to broaden the student's reasoning and lead him/her to question the validity of his/her own beliefs and mental schemes in relation to this physical reality.

    The main objective is learning the fundamentals of electronics and its relationship with the physical operation of computers, peripherals and communication systems used in computing, being able to apply digital logic design techniques for creating simple digital circuits.

  • The Company and Its Processes

    The topics of this subject aim to place the student in the business environment, with special attention to the information processes that take place in any organization. This subject allows the student to mature their abilities of understanding and reasoning of the reality of the ICT role in businesses, through which they will learn to develop and deepen the critical vision of the world around them, the ultimate goal being to achieve true and sensible thinking in a business environment.

  • History of Science and Technology

    The aim is for the student, at the beginning of their studies, to have a panoramic view of the most significant events that have marked the history of their own culture, and particularly, the role that scientific development has played in accelerating modern history. It is also intended that the student situates their profession and thus gains historical awareness of their own field of knowledge, its common roots with philosophy, mathematics, and physics, and its subsequent evolution linked to machine manufacturing, technology, and the construction of systems.
